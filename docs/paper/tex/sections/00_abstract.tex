\begin{abstract}
\noindent
XLM-R over-fragments tokens of unseen tail languages (tokens/word = 2.204).
Glot500 extends XLM-R's vocabulary with a mixed-corpus SentencePiece tokenizer and
continued MLM pretraining, but leaves the initialization of new token embedding rows open.
We fill this gap with a controlled 5-way ablation on \texttt{xlm-roberta-base}:
tokenizer fixed to Glot500-style append (116,664 new pieces), only the initialization varies---
\rand, \meanm, \fvt, \wfvt, and \famm\ (family-aware mean)---across 50K steps on
92 head and 7 tail languages (Target7).

Three findings emerge.
(1) The extended tokenizer reduces Target7 tokens/word by 27.75\% (2.204 $\to$ 1.592).
(2) Initialization alone shifts convergence: \famm\ achieves the lowest tail PPPL (12.3) and
Tatoeba tail accuracy (36.1); \wfvt\ leads on training loss (2.73), head/all PPPL, Bible, and
roundtrip alignment; FVT-family methods lead at 10K but are overtaken by \wfvt\ and \famm\ by 30K.
(3) The representation space exhibits family/typology clustering; languages with strong head-set
family coverage benefit most from \famm.
Embedding initialization is a consequential design choice for low-resource vocabulary adaptation.
\end{abstract}
