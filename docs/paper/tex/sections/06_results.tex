\section{Results}

\subsection{Tokenizer Fertility}

Applying the extended tokenizer to 3,500 Target7 sentences (500 per language) reduces
tokens/word from 2.204 to 1.592, a 27.75\% decrease.
Per-language reductions range from 34.9\% (\texttt{xav}) and 33.7\% (\texttt{csb})
down to 12.4\% (\texttt{ile}).
Because all five initialization variants share this tokenizer, subsequent
method-to-method differences cannot be attributed to tokenizer fertility.

\begin{figure}[h]\centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.82\linewidth]{tokenization_effect_change.png}
  \caption{Tokenizer fertility reduction for Target7.
           The extended tokenizer reduces tokens/word by 27.75\% on average.
           All five initialization methods share this tokenizer.}
  \label{fig:tokenization}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Per-Language PPPL Breakdown for \famm}

Table~\ref{tab:pppl_lang} decomposes the \famm\ tail PPPL advantage by language at 50K.

\begin{table}[h]\centering\small
\caption{Per-language PPPL at 50K (lower is better).
         ``prior'' = number of same-family head languages available to \famm.
         \textbf{Bold} = row minimum.}
\label{tab:pppl_lang}
\begin{tabular}{@{}llcrrrrr@{}}
\toprule
Language & Family & prior & \rand & \fvt & \wfvt & \famm \\
\midrule
\texttt{dtp} & Austronesian & 5 & 127.4 & 245.3 & 157.7 & \textbf{41.2} \\
\texttt{csb} & Slavic       & 5 &  42.7 &  25.0 & \textbf{20.5} & 20.7 \\
\texttt{fur} & Romance      & 5 &  54.5 &  38.5 &  31.6 & \textbf{28.8} \\
\texttt{lij} & Romance      & 5 &  30.4 &  22.9 &  20.2 & \textbf{18.1} \\
\texttt{ile} & Constructed  & 1 &  10.5 &   7.4 & \textbf{6.9} &  7.1 \\
\texttt{bam} & Mande        & proxy & 144.7 & 115.9 & 97.8 & \textbf{78.0} \\
\texttt{xav} & Macro-Je     & none &  2.7 &   2.6 & \textbf{2.3} & \textbf{2.3} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Three patterns emerge.
First, \texttt{dtp} (Austronesian) accounts for the bulk of \famm's aggregate advantage.
Five Austronesian head languages (\texttt{ind/msa/jav/tgl/mlg}) provide a strong family prior,
and \famm\ converges to PPPL 41.2 versus \fvt's 245.3---a 6$\times$ gap.
Notably, \fvt\ (245.3) is worse than \rand\ (127.4) for \texttt{dtp}:
the XLM-R source tokenizer decomposes \texttt{dtp} tokens into pieces that carry
European semantic signal, making surface-form prior \emph{harmful} relative to
random initialization for this language.
\wfvt\ (157.7) is likewise worse than \rand.

Second, Romance (\texttt{fur, lij}) shows clear \famm\ gains of 25\% and 21\% over \fvt,
while Slavic (\texttt{csb}) and Constructed (\texttt{ile}) show \wfvt\ and \famm\ within
0.2 of each other---effectively tied.

Third, even languages without a direct family prior benefit from \famm.
\texttt{bam} (Mande) uses a Niger-Congo proxy (\texttt{xho}) and still achieves PPPL 78.0
versus \fvt's 115.9 (33\% lower).
\texttt{xav} (Macro-Je) has no usable prior; all methods converge to the 2.3--2.7 range
with differences below 0.4.

In sum, \famm's aggregate tail PPPL advantage is concentrated in \texttt{dtp}, confirming
the prediction in Table~\ref{tab:fam}: the larger the family coverage in the head set,
the greater the benefit of family-aware initialization.

\subsection{Loss Convergence}

Figure~\ref{fig:loss} shows MLM training loss for all five methods over 50K steps.
Final losses are: \wfvt\ 2.73 $<$ \fvt\ 2.76 $<$ \famm\ 2.91 $<$ \rand\ 3.12 $<$ \meanm\ 3.27.
Despite sharing the same tokenizer, corpus, and objective, the five methods converge to
distinct loss levels.
FVT-family methods reach the lowest loss; \meanm\ ends higher than \rand.
Collapsing all new token rows to a single centroid (\meanm) hinders gradient separation
in the early steps, producing persistent disadvantage.

\begin{figure}[h]\centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.82\linewidth]{convergence_5way_loss_curve.png}
  \caption{5-way MLM training loss (50K steps).
           FVT-family methods converge lowest; \meanm\ converges worst.
           The ranking is stable from early training through convergence.}
  \label{fig:loss}
\end{figure}

evolve from 10K to 50K steps.

\fvt\ starts with the best tail PPPL at 10K (21.2) but plateaus thereafter.
\wfvt\ and \famm\ start lower at 10K (26.9 and 34.2), improve continuously, and
overtake \fvt\ by 30K---a clear crossover.
\rand\ and \meanm\ remain at the bottom throughout.
\textbf{The benefit of a structured initialization prior is visible at convergence,
not at early checkpoints.}
Evaluating initialization methods from early diagnostics alone would rank \fvt\ first and
miss the eventual superiority of \wfvt\ and \famm.
This crossover is consistent with the loss ordering in Figure~\ref{fig:loss}.
